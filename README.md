## Reflection

Software development is inherently collaborative. *Documentation* and *diagrams* are the 'source of truth', that make the architectural intent clear to everyone in the team. *Version control* and *automated tests* safeguard the codebase, helping team members to experiment without fearing they'll break important features. 

Combining *pair programming* and *peer reviews*, we go from **individual work** to actual **shared code ownership**. This helps the team understand the reasoning behind the code by making technical challenges into collective knowledge. 

These tools help us have a culture of accountability where everyone is invested in the **project's quality** and **long-term maintainability**. And of course makes the work environment healthier by ensuring the code is easier to understand at a first glance, without having to ask or being frustrated by the team's lack of communication.
